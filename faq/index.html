FAQ

Questions parents ask most

Honest, reassuring answers. If your question isn't here, please reach out — no question is too small.

What is a mohel, and how is it different from a doctor performing the procedure?

A mohel is a trained specialist who performs the bris both as a religious ceremony and as a careful medical procedure. Unlike a hospital circumcision, a brit milah is a sacred ritual performed on the eighth day — wrapped in blessings, family honors, and your son's naming. A mohel performs this specific procedure regularly, with reverence and skill.

Is it painful for the baby?

The procedure is very quick — usually only five to ten seconds — and uses a gentle, time-tested technique. No injections or drugs are needed, and a topical numbing cream is available on request. Most babies settle within moments, and many sleep right through the celebration afterward.

How is the bris performed, and is there a lot of bleeding?

I use the traditional Mogen shield method — a shielded technique refined over generations that is quick and precise. Because the procedure takes only seconds and the eighth-day timing supports your baby's natural clotting, bleeding is minimal and recovery is typically fast and smooth.

What if the baby isn't ready on day eight (medical reasons)?

Your baby's health always comes first. If your pediatrician advises waiting — for jaundice, prematurity, or any other reason — we simply reschedule for when your son is strong and ready. I'll help you determine the right timing.

Do you serve Reform, Conservative, and Orthodox families?

Yes, warmly. I work with families across the full spectrum of Jewish life — including interfaith and secular families — and tailor the ceremony to your traditions and level of comfort.

How far in advance should we book?

As early as you can — even before the baby arrives. Because the eighth day comes quickly, don't hesitate to reach out right after the birth. I keep time available for new families.

What happens during the ceremony?

Your baby is welcomed, the brief circumcision is performed gently, blessings are recited over wine and the covenant, and your son receives his Hebrew name. It concludes with a festive meal. The ceremony itself takes only a few minutes. You can read a fuller walkthrough on The Bris Process page.

Do you travel to our home or synagogue?

Yes. A bris does not need to take place in a synagogue — many families choose the comfort and intimacy of their own home. I come to you, performing the bris in your home, at your synagogue, or at another venue of your choosing, throughout Greater Cleveland and Northeast Ohio. I bring everything needed for the ceremony.

Still have a question?
