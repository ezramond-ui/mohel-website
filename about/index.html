About the Mohel

Rabbi Ezra Mond

Rabbi Ezra Mond has always felt a natural connection to the mitzvah of bris milah. As the oldest of ten brothers, he grew up with years of firsthand exposure to newborns and the special role of a mohel in welcoming a child into Klal Yisrael. Combined with his longtime interest in medicine, attention to detail, and steady hands, milah became the natural path for him.

Training & experience

Rabbi Mond trained under leading mohalim in Lakewood, New Jersey, and continued advanced training in Monroe, New York. He has also developed relationships with experienced physicians who have provided valuable medical guidance and support.

  • Trained under leading mohalim in Lakewood, New Jersey
  • Advanced training in Monroe, New York
  • Ongoing medical guidance from experienced physicians
  • Committed to the highest halachic standards and the timeless mesorah
  • Traditional Mogen shield method, with modern medical safety

Philosophy & approach

With a commitment to the highest halachic standards and the timeless mesorah of generations, Rabbi Mond performs each bris with meticulous care, combining traditional practice with modern medical safety. His goal is to provide families with a bris that reflects the beauty of the mitzvah while offering confidence, comfort, and precision.

Each bris is performed with meticulous care — joining the beauty of tradition with the confidence of modern medical safety.

Serving every kind of family

I have the honor of working with families across the full spectrum of Jewish life — observant and secular, interfaith and multigenerational. Wherever you are on your journey, you'll be met with warmth and without judgment. Learn more about the communities I serve or read what to expect at a bris.
